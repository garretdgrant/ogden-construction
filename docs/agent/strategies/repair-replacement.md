Quick Answer: It Depends on Safety, Structure, and Cost

Bullet points:

- Minor surface issues → repair
- Structural or widespread damage → replacement
- Safety should always come first

Avoid absolutes.

---

## SECTION: WHEN DECK REPAIR MAKES SENSE

H2:
When Repairing a Deck Is the Right Choice

Bullet points:

- Cosmetic issues (loose boards, splinters)
- Isolated damage
- Deck is structurally sound
- Deck is relatively new

Mention:
Repairs should never compromise safety.

---

## SECTION: WHEN DECK REPLACEMENT IS THE BETTER OPTION

H2:
When It’s Time to Replace a Deck

Bullet points:

- Rotting or soft framing
- Failing posts or footings
- Code violations
- Extensive repairs adding up
- Deck age (15–20+ years depending on materials)

Mention:
Older decks often don’t meet current safety standards.

---

## SECTION: SAFETY CONCERNS (IMPORTANT)

H2:
Deck Safety: Why Replacement Is Sometimes Necessary

- Structural integrity
- Load-bearing concerns
- Railing height and spacing
- Stair safety

This section builds urgency without fear tactics.
